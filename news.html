Meet Maya, Tara & Raj

Maya, Tara, and Raj were born in early December 2015. ISDF’s dear rescue friend, Mini Seth Kapoor, saw the puppies with mom when they were quite young. She dreamed of rescuing all five, but knew the situation in Delhi made this mere fantasy – so many dogs in need, so few homes for them all. Mini also lacked the funds for overseas travel and adoption at the time, due to her dedicated work helping so many of the Delhi street dogs.

Soi Dog is coming to Korea! They have short and long term plans working on the government level. Soi Dog sterilization program is supporting our spaying/neutering the dogs and this time with the bus ad. This banner will be on the both side of line 88 buses from the 27th August. 13 buses all together running the streets of Bucheon, Incheon and Seoul till next year August 2017. Bucheon city is our target. Thank you sponsors! Translated, the ads are carrying this message: “Dog! Dogs are not for eating – they are our companions. There is no differentiation between a ‘pet dog’ and a ‘meat dog’. How would you feel if your pet ended up in a slaughterhouse?” The Soi Dog Foundation is the largest animal welfare agency in SE Asia based in Thailand. They have changed the landscape for dogs in Thailand and have had tremendous impact throughout SE Asia. They have virtually ended the dog meat trade in Thailand, helped to enact and enforce animal welfare laws in Thailand and other SE Asia countries. They have curbed the tide of street dogs through effective spay and neuter programs. This is an organization that is worthy of our utmost respect and admiration. It was all started by John and Gill Dailey – two people with a passion and a strong determination. Never underestimate your abilities and how your actions will spread and multiply to bring about change and good in the world.

155 mile marathon across the Gobi Desert and a very determined dog. He followed a runner, Dion Leonard, the entire way and in doing so stole his heart and ended up forming a bond that led to him joining Dion in the UK. For those of you who have traveled abroad and had a street dog speak to your heart knows how hard it is to part ways. So desperate for human attention they will follow you as long as you let them even if it means taking them out of their safety zone into other dog’s territories which can be very unsafe for them. Many a street dogs have left lasting memories to the tourists that visit their countries and broken hearts when they had to part. I often receive calls from tourists who fell in love with a dog while abroad. Here is what I tell them “Where there is a will there is a way. Take them home and you will never regret it”
